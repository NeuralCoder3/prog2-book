Item 1.

We introduce a small example (abstract syntax of a small expression language) and model the data structure in a functional, imperative, and OO language. Functional languages have algebraic data types and matching that make the life of the programmer easy. In C one needs to literally implement tagged unions and do the case distinctions on a very low level. In OO, each variant becomes a class and the name of the ADT is typically an interface. The cases of the functional and imperative programs become individual methods in the respective classes.
