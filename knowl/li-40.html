Item 3.

R-evaluating the expression 2 gives the value \(2\text{.}\) The left side of the assignment is more exciting: The expression *y is, by definition, L-evaluable; otherwise it could not occur on the left side of an assignment. Its value is the result of the R-evaluation of its operand. Here, this is the content of the container of y: the address of x. Therefore, the value \(2\) is written to the container bound to x.
