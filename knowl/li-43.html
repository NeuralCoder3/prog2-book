Item 6.

The interesting part is the occurrence of the dereference operator on the right side: R-evaluating y yields the content of y's container, i.e. the address of x. We have seen that the expression *y is L-evaluable. The R-evaluation of an L-evaluable expression just reads the contents of the container whose address results from the L-evaluation. In this example, that is the content of the container of x, \(3\text{.}\) In the end, w therefore carries the value \(3\text{.}\)
